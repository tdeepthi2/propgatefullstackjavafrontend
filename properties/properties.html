About Properties Entity

The Property entity represents physical real estate assets managed within PropGate. Each property belongs to an owner (linked via `owner_id`) and serves as the core financial asset. Financial performance statistics, expenses, and rental income cash flows are tied back to specific property units.

Property Classification:

Real estate assets are grouped into three primary classes. Residential properties consist of single-family homes, multi-family complexes, apartments, and condominiums. Commercial properties comprise office spaces, retail buildings, medical centers, warehouses, and industrial parks. Land records account for raw agricultural tracts, residential subdivision lots, and commercial zoning sections.

Property Financial Calculations & Logic

Real estate performance in PropGate depends on mathematical metrics derived directly from individual Property settings. The Net Capital Gain / Appreciation is computed as currentValue - purchasePrice to track total portfolio asset growth. The annual Rental Yield (%) is calculated as (rentalIncome * 12) / purchasePrice * 100 to evaluate relative rental payout productivity against initial deployment costs. Finally, a property record acts as the root Associated Asset container linking corresponding transaction records, rent payment histories, and appreciation schedule records.
